\begin{abstract}
Forests regulate moisture and heat fluxes in the lower atmosphere, which are inextricably linked to fair weather shallow cumulus formation within the lowest level of the atmosphere. Forest-enhanced cloud shading then affects the earth's surface radiation budget, which has been reasonably well studied using computational methods and satellite data. However, there is a lack of surface-based observational studies linking the exchange of energy and matter at the surface to low-level cloud formation. The dataset available combines surface-based flux measurements with ceilometer data, obtained from the Acadia Research Forest monitoring site near Fredericton, NB.
